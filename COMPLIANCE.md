# GMI‑VS Compliance Framework

## Governance and Compliance

**NovaFuse‑GMI‑VS** implements a formal governance and compliance framework designed to support verification standard integrity, auditability, and regulatory alignment.

---

## Governance Structure

### NovaFuse Technologies — Standard Steward

- **Specification Authority**  
  Maintains the GMI‑VS mathematical invariants and normative requirements.

- **Version Control**  
  Manages semantic versioning, change control, and backward compatibility.

- **Compliance Oversight**  
  Oversees independent auditability and validation processes.

- **Community Review**  
  Publishes the specification openly for public review and comment.

---

### Chain of Custody

- **Development**  
  NovaFuse research and development team.

- **Implementation**  
  Verification engine, adversarial test suites, and certification logic.

- **Validation**  
  Independent security and integrity review.

- **Deployment**  
  Automated build, verification, and release management.

- **Operations**  
  Continuous monitoring and incident response.

---

## Compliance Requirements

### Technical Compliance

- **Mathematical Invariance**  
  All defined adversarial scenarios must pass invariant verification.

- **Deterministic Results**  
  Verification outcomes must be reproducible and repeatable.

- **Evidence Preservation**  
  Complete audit trail with cryptographic integrity proofs.

- **Artifact Generation**  
  Structured outputs including JSON, CSV, HTML, and certificates.

- **Version Control**  
  Semantic versioning with traceable change history.

---

### Regulatory Alignment

- **ISO/IEC 27001**  
  Information security management alignment.

- **NIST SP 800‑171**  
  Security and privacy control implementation guidance.

- **SOC 2 Type III**  
  Cryptographic integrity and security control requirements.

- **GDPR Article 25**  
  Data protection and privacy by design principles.
